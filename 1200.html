Overview

The 1200s marked significant political and cultural changes in Europe, including the rise of universities and the establishment of powerful monarchies.

Key Events

  • 1204: The Fourth Crusade results in the sack of Constantinople, significantly impacting the Byzantine Empire.
  • 1206: Genghis Khan unifies the Mongol tribes, beginning the expansion of the Mongol Empire.
  • 1215: The Magna Carta is signed in England, establishing the principle of legal limits on royal power.
  • 1220: The University of Paris is formally established, becoming a major center for learning in Europe.
