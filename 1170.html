Decade: 1170

Overview

The 1170s were marked by significant political changes and religious conflicts, further shaping the landscape of medieval Europe.

Key Events

  • 1170: The assassination of Thomas Becket, Archbishop of Canterbury.
  • 1171: Henry II of England invades Ireland, marking the beginning of English involvement in Ireland.
  • 1174: The Treaty of Falaise is signed, securing Scottish loyalty to England.
  • 1179: The third Lateran Council is convened, addressing various issues within the Church.
