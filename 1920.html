1920

Overview

The year 1920 was notable for the end of World War I and the beginning of significant social changes, including women's suffrage in the United States.

Key Events

  • The League of Nations Established: The League of Nations is founded to promote peace and cooperation between countries.
  • Women's Suffrage in the US: The 19th Amendment is ratified, granting women the right to vote in the United States.
  • Prohibition Begins: The 18th Amendment prohibits the manufacture, sale, and transportation of alcoholic beverages in the US.
  • First Radio Broadcast: The first radio news program is broadcast in the United States, marking a new era in communication.
